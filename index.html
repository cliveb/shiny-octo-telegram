The iconic Californian festival makes an eagerly awaited return

Coachella is North America's most famous music festival, held each April at its now iconic home at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, California.

A festival on every true music fan's bucket list, Coachella will make its eagerly anticipated return in April 2021 after a year off, once again returning with a world-beating lineup of some of music's biggest stars, covering a range of genres, including rock, indie, hip-hop and dance.

Coachella's illustrious history has seen it feature legendary headline sets from the likes of Beyoncé, Daft Punk, Prince, Radiohead, and Bob Dylan over its two decade-long lifespan.

FAQs

BY PLANE:

The closest airports to the festival are Palm Springs, Los Angeles and Ontario. 

The nearest airport is Palm Springs International Airport (PSP). If you are flying into here, take the 30 bus to B St at Buddy Rogers (28 minutes). Here transfer to the 111 bus and get off at Hwy 111 at Madison (57 minutes). Follow signs to the festival site from the bus stop.

There is also a shuttle bus system between Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) and the festival site. Please note that these shuttle services are not in any way connected with Festicket. More information can be found on the Coachella website. 

As well, there is an "Any Line, Anytime" shuttle system stopping at various hotels. More information can be found on the Coachella website. 

BY CAR:

If you are driving to Coachella, you will want to enter the Empire Polo Club (81-800 Avenue 51, Indio, CA 92201) onto your sat nav. Average driving times are as follows:

From Los Angeles - Take the I-10 E (2 hours)

From San Diego - Take the I-15 N (2 hours 45 minutes)

From Las Vegas - Take the I-15 S (4 hours 30 minutes)

From San Francisco - Take the I-5 S (8 hours 30 minutes)

This is an all ages festival.
